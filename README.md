## Public work to start with

### Transit data, policy, and public delivery

- **[Transit Delivery Atlas](https://github.com/ChelseaKR/transit-delivery-atlas)** — A source-linked
  crosswalk of California transit directives that keeps signed source facts, analytical delivery
  relationships, and open questions visibly separate. [Explore the atlas](https://transit.chelseakr.com).
- **[GTFS Scorecard](https://github.com/ChelseaKR/gtfs-scorecard)** — Live, plain-language quality
  scorecards for 1,100+ U.S. and Canadian GTFS feeds, with GTFS-Realtime monitoring where available,
  a fail-closed CI gate, and a read-only MCP server. [Visit gtfsscorecard.org](https://gtfsscorecard.org).

  validator for the CLI, GitHub Actions, pre-commit, browser, Docker, and LSP workflows. It includes
  auto-fix, GTFS drift diagnostics, and a
  [merged upstream standards correction](https://github.com/MobilityData/transit-operational-data-standard/pull/147).
- **[Fare Policy Assistant](https://github.com/ChelseaKR/fare-policy-assistant)** — A reduced-fare
  reference assistant for five California transit agencies, designed for cited English and Spanish
  answers. It never determines eligibility and ships with a public 201-case evaluation plus a
  separate black-box audit.
- **[NearMiss](https://github.com/ChelseaKR/nearmiss)** — Reproducible road-hazard analysis with
  exposure normalization, uncertainty, known-answer hotspot benchmarks, multi-source ingestion,
  and a QGIS plugin. Its public demos use synthetic data and say so.

## My ethics are implementation constraints

Technology is not neutral, and an ethics statement that never changes a design—or a yes/no
decision—is just branding.

- I do not pursue work in defense, weapons, or warfare; law enforcement, policing, or mass
  surveillance; or the prison-industrial complex and technologies that profit from incarceration.
  I also do not work with organizations based in, or strongly tied to, Israel.
- I prioritize mission-driven organizations that serve underserved communities and practice
  diverse leadership.
- I build AI as inspectable decision support, not as an automated judge of people. High-stakes
  decisions stay with accountable humans, with evidence, rationale, limitations, and a correction
  path visible at the point of use.
- I design for the people most affected to keep agency over their information: collect less,
  prefer local-first and offline-capable systems, make disclosure consent-based, and treat privacy,
  accessibility, and bilingual access as architecture—not polish.
- I keep source facts, calculations, and analytical judgment separate; prefer reproducible evidence
  and known-answer tests over persuasive demos; and treat uncertainty and negative findings as
  product requirements.

## How I build

I work across Python, TypeScript, React, AWS, data systems, platform engineering, and applied AI,
but the durable part is the operating discipline: explicit system boundaries, threat models,
provenance, measurable acceptance criteria, accessible interfaces, observable services, and
fail-closed checks where a confident wrong answer would cause harm.

I use AI agents as implementation collaborators while retaining responsibility for architecture,
standards, evaluation design, security and privacy decisions, acceptance criteria, and releases.
Legal, policy, subject-matter, and manual accessibility reviews remain human gates and are labeled
when pending.
